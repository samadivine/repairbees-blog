# Brand Voice Guidelines

## Author
Sama Divine

## Tone
- **Clear and practical** — Write like you're helping a neighbour solve a real problem, not lecturing
- **Problem-solving** — Every article should leave the reader knowing exactly what to do next
- **Trustworthy** — Homeowners are often stressed about plumbing and repairs. Be the calm, knowledgeable friend
- **Direct** — Get to the point quickly, respect the reader's time and urgency
- **Evidence-backed** — Take a stance, but back it up with data, costs, or first-hand experience

## Writing Style
- Use "you" and "your" to speak directly to the reader
- Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences max, hard limit 4 sentences)
- Use step-by-step instructions where relevant (real, practical steps)
- Include cost estimates and time frames where appropriate
- Avoid jargon unless the audience expects it (then define it on first use)
- Use analogies to explain complex plumbing and repair concepts
- Active voice preferred over passive (keep passive voice under 10% of sentences)
- Lead with the answer, then explain — show then tell, not tell then show

## Sentence Rules
- Average sentence length: 15–20 words
- Maximum sentence length: 25 words
- No more than 25% of sentences over 20 words
- Vary sentence length — mix short punchy sentences (5–10 words) with medium (15–20 words)
- No more than 3 consecutive sentences within 5 words of each other's length
- Uniform sentence length signals AI authorship — human writing has natural burstiness

## Paragraph Rules
- Target 40–80 words per paragraph
- Hard limit: 150 words per paragraph
- Maximum 2–3 sentences per paragraph (single-sentence paragraphs are fine for emphasis)
- Start each paragraph with the most important sentence
- One topic per paragraph — no topic drift

## Readability Targets
- Flesch Reading Ease: 60–70 (optimal for both engagement and AI citation)
- Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level: 7–8
- Conversational authority: natural language that mirrors how experts actually explain things

## Audience
- Homeowners dealing with plumbing problems and home repairs
- Tenants who need to understand what's wrong before calling a landlord or plumber
- Property managers and landlords maintaining rental properties
- DIY-minded people who want to fix simple issues themselves
- People dealing with a plumbing emergency who need quick, reliable advice

## Topics We Cover
- Plumbing problems and fixes (leaks, clogs, burst pipes, low pressure)
- Home repair guides (step-by-step tutorials for common household issues)
- Emergency fixes (what to do right now before a plumber arrives)
- Maintenance tips (preventive schedules, seasonal checklists, inspections)
- Cost guides (how much repairs cost, DIY vs professional, budgeting)

## What We DON'T Do
- Generic "call a plumber" advice without actionable DIY steps first
- Content that reads like it was written by AI
- Clickbait without substance
- Promoting products or services we haven't actually evaluated
- Overly technical trade-level writing that intimidates homeowners

## Experience Signals (E-E-A-T)
Use these patterns to demonstrate first-hand expertise:
- "When I tested..." — experiment or hands-on context
- "In my experience..." — practitioner insight
- "After using this for [N] weeks..." — long-term observation
- "Here's what most plumbers won't tell you..." — insider knowledge
- "The mistake most homeowners make is..." — practitioner wisdom
- "What the manufacturer instructions don't mention is..." — insider knowledge

### Anti-Patterns (NEVER use these)
- "As an expert in..." — self-declared without evidence
- "It is well known that..." — vague consensus
- "Studies show..." (unsourced) — authority claim without citation
- "Everyone agrees..." — unfalsifiable statement
- "Trust me when I say..." — demanding rather than earning trust

## Banned Language

### AI Trigger Words (NEVER use)
delve, tapestry, robust, leverage, utilize, comprehensive, landscape,
crucial, furthermore, indeed, moreover, paramount, facilitate,
harness, unleash, game-changer, revolutionary

### Corporate / Hype Phrases (NEVER use)
- "We're excited to announce"
- "Best-in-class", "industry-leading", "world-class"
- "Seamless", "empower", "unlock", "robust"
- "At Repairbees, we believe..."
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape"
- "In this blog post, we will explore..."

### Filler Transitions (NEVER use)
- "That being said"
- "Without further ado"
- "As you might know"
- "It goes without saying"
- "As mentioned earlier"
- "Moving on to the next section"

### Unsupported Claims (NEVER use without a specific number)
- "significantly faster/better/more"
- "massive improvement"
- "huge difference"
- Replace EVERY magnitude adjective with a specific number — if no number exists, remove the claim

## Self-Promotion Rules
- Maximum 1 brand mention per article (author bio context only)
- Remove "At Repairbees, we..." patterns
- CTAs should feel helpful, not salesy
- Author section demonstrates credentials through experience, not marketing language

## Information Gain — The Differentiator
Every article must provide value AI cannot independently create:
- **Personal experience**: Opinions and observations from actual plumbing and repair work
- **Original testing**: Photos, measurements, real results from actual repairs
- **Process documentation**: "Here's exactly what I did" walkthroughs
- **Practitioner wisdom**: Tips that only come from real-world experience
- **Cost specifics**: Real price ranges based on actual quotes and invoices
